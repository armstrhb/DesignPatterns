The Builder Pattern

Added Sun, Mar 29 2015
  • creational

Sometimes object creation isn’t simple. Why not let something do it for you?

Explained as to a 5 year old

You’re great at drawing pictures. A friend lets you know that they would like to have a picture of a dinosaur. So, you sit down at a table with some paper and crayons, and draw a picture of a dinosaur. Very nice!

A few moments later, another friend asks for a picture of of a firetruck. Again, you sit down and whip up a picture of a firetruck.

You’re acting out the builder pattern. Think of the pictures as objects. You, as the builder, receive some sort of input (like a friend saying they want some type of picture). You take care of the creation of the object (gathering the required drawing materials, paper, drawing the picture, etc.) and return it to the requester.
